Three ways to sound like 2006

Y2K wasn't one style — it was three dialects living on the same internet. Your two references point at two different ones. Each direction below is a complete, buildable system: palette, type stack (all era-authentic system fonts — zero webfont loading, exactly like 2006), and a rendered UI sample.

1 · Chrome & Acid Recommended

Winamp, the innioasis iPod theme, MP3-player packaging. Brushed-metal silver against near-black, with an acid green that doubles as a wink at Spotify's real brand color. Dark UI = album art pops, and the LCD-green display is instantly readable as "music hardware."

Studio recommendation: Chrome & Acid shell, Scrapbook garnish

Use Direction 1 as the app's design system — the shell, player, library, and buttons. It's the only direction that connects both of your references and Spotify's brand identity in one move, it keeps album art vivid on dark ground, and dark chrome stays usable at real information density (a music library is a data-heavy UI).

Then borrow from Direction 3 for personality moments: the AI buddy's IM window, playlist "mix CD" covers, and profile-ish flourishes get stickers, polaroids, and Comic Sans. That contrast — disciplined hardware chrome vs. scrapbook chaos in the social corners — is exactly how 2006 actually looked, and it gives judges both authenticity and a creativity story.

Aero glass we save for one cameo: glossy pill buttons on dialogs/tooltips, so the era trifecta is represented without three systems fighting.
